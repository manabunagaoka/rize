## 1. EDUCATION: Project Health & Literacy (PH&L)

**Organization:** Project Health & Literacy  
**Founded:** 2009  
**Founder(s):** Harvard Medical School students (originally founded by HMS students)  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Harvard Medical School student organization

**Mission:**  
Empower underserved communities through health education and literacy programs. Medical students teach health literacy in local schools, homeless shelters, and community centers.

**Impact Metrics:**
- Serves 1,000+ students annually in Boston-area schools
- 100+ Harvard medical student volunteers
- Health literacy curriculum in 15+ partner schools
- Focus on diabetes prevention, nutrition, mental health awareness

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"Project Health & Literacy bridges the gap between medical expertise and community need. Harvard medical students deliver evidence-based health education to underserved youth, creating a generation of health-literate citizens while training compassionate physicians. Every dollar invested expands our reach to more classrooms, more communities, more lives changed through the power of health knowledge."

**Category:** Education  
**Contact:** Harvard Medical School student organizations

## 2. HEALTHCARE: Health Leads

**Organization:** Health Leads  
**Founded:** 1996 (at Harvard)  
**Founder(s):** Rebecca Onie (Harvard College '97)  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Founded by Harvard undergraduate, grew from Harvard innovation

**Mission:**  
Address social determinants of health by connecting patients with essential resources like food, housing, and utilities. Pioneered "social prescribing" in healthcare.

**Impact Metrics:**
- Operates in 30+ healthcare facilities nationwide
- Served 500,000+ patients
- Connected patients to $500M+ in social resources
- Trained 10,000+ college student advocates
- MacArthur "Genius Grant" to founder

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"Health Leads revolutionizes healthcare by treating more than symptoms—we treat the conditions that cause them. Born at Harvard, we've proven that addressing food insecurity, housing instability, and poverty is as critical as prescribing medication. Invest in the future of holistic healthcare where every patient receives not just treatment, but the resources to truly heal."

**Category:** Healthcare  
**Website:** healthleadsusa.org

## 3. CLIMATE: Harvard Square Climate Action

**Organization:** Harvard Square Climate Action Initiative  
**Founded:** 2019  
**Founder(s):** Harvard undergraduates concerned with campus sustainability  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Harvard student organization

**Mission:**  
Drive local climate action through community organizing, policy advocacy, and sustainable business partnerships in Cambridge. Focus on making Harvard Square carbon-neutral by 2030.

**Impact Metrics:**
- Partnered with 25+ local businesses for sustainability commitments
- Organized 500+ students for climate advocacy
- Achieved 15% emissions reduction in partner businesses
- Influenced Harvard's $42B endowment divestment from fossil fuels
- Created Cambridge's first student-led climate action network

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"Harvard Square Climate Action proves that local action creates global impact. We mobilize Harvard students to transform our neighborhood into a living laboratory for climate solutions—from zero-waste restaurants to renewable energy partnerships. Your investment helps us scale this model to college towns nationwide, showing the world that students can lead the climate revolution."

**Category:** Climate  
**Contact:** Harvard Office for Sustainability partnerships

## 5. GLOBAL POVERTY: Harvard Square Homeless Shelter

**Organization:** Harvard Square Homeless Shelter (HSHS)  
**Founded:** 1983  
**Founder(s):** Harvard and MIT students  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Jointly run by Harvard and MIT students

**Mission:**  
Student-run emergency homeless shelter providing 24-hour care, meals, case management, and housing placement for homeless individuals in Cambridge during winter months.

**Impact Metrics:**
- Operates 6 months/year (November-April)
- 22 beds nightly
- 1,000+ shelter nights provided annually
- 200+ student volunteers
- 70% of guests transition to permanent housing
- Entirely funded by student fundraising and donations

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"The Harvard Square Homeless Shelter is proof that students can run life-saving services. For 42 years, Harvard and MIT undergrads have provided warmth, meals, and dignity to Cambridge's homeless—every night, all winter, with zero government funding. We're more than a shelter; we're a training ground for compassionate leadership. Your investment keeps our doors open and our guests on the path to permanent housing."

**Category:** Global Poverty (local poverty alleviation)  
**Website:** hshs.org

## 6. MENTAL HEALTH: Room 13

**Organization:** Room 13  
**Founded:** 2016  
**Founder(s):** Harvard undergraduates focused on mental health peer support  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Harvard student organization

**Mission:**  
Combat mental health stigma and provide peer-to-peer support for Harvard students. Create safe spaces for vulnerable conversations about depression, anxiety, and suicide prevention.

**Impact Metrics:**
- 500+ peer counseling sessions annually
- Trained 100+ student peer counselors
- Mental health awareness events reaching 2,000+ students
- Reduced mental health-related leaves by 12% (estimated impact)
- Partnership with Harvard University Health Services
- Anonymous support hotline staffed by students

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"Room 13 exists because sometimes the best person to talk to isn't a professional—it's someone who gets it. We're Harvard students helping Harvard students survive the pressure, the loneliness, the darkness. Our peer counselors are trained, our approach is evidence-based, and our impact is life-saving. Invest in mental health support that meets students where they are, delivered by people who've been there too."

**Category:** Mental Health  
**Contact:** Harvard student mental health initiatives

## 7. FOOD SECURITY: Harvard Food Literacy Project (HFLP)

**Organization:** Harvard Food Literacy Project  
**Founded:** 2012  
**Founder(s):** Harvard undergraduates from School of Public Health partnerships  
**Harvard Affiliation:** Harvard student organization with HSPH collaboration

**Mission:**  
Teach cooking skills, nutrition education, and food security awareness to low-income families and children in Boston. Address food deserts through mobile cooking classes and community gardens.

**Impact Metrics:**
- 1,500+ participants in cooking classes (2024)
- 8 community gardens established in food deserts
- 75+ Harvard student volunteers and instructors
- Distributed 20,000 lbs of fresh produce annually
- Partnerships with 12 Boston community centers
- Reduced food insecurity in partner communities by 18%

**Elevator Pitch:**  
"Harvard Food Literacy Project fights hunger with knowledge and empowerment. We teach low-income families to cook nutritious meals on a budget, grow their own food, and advocate for food justice in their communities. Our mobile cooking classes bring culinary education directly to food deserts. Invest in a future where everyone has access not just to food, but to the skills and dignity of feeding themselves well."

**Category:** Food Security  
**Contact:** Harvard School of Public Health partnerships
